What We Do

A breakdown of our club structure!

Our entire studio located at Trafalgar Campus is wheelchair accessible and health & safety inspected each month, ensuring a comfortable and inclusive experience for all artists. Accessible washrooms are also located nearby.

Open Studio Time


Price Range: No entrance fee

Only need to pay for weight of clay used

Open to: Members-only (No Registration Needed)

When: Twice A Week

This is your dedicated time to work independently, practice techniques, and complete your projects at your own pace.


Practice & Production:

Open Studio is available for all members, offering access to pottery wheels, large work tables for hand-building, and all necessary tools.


Resource Access:

Use our specialized equipment, including various glazes and kiln space for firing your finished pieces (details on firing fees and procedures are available to members).


Flexible Scheduling:

Open Studio sessions are held twice a week during convenient evening hours to accommodate different schedules.

Workshops


Price Range: $10-$15
Open to: Members-only (Must Register to Attend)

When: Bi-Weekly

Designed for both the total beginner and the advanced ceramicist, our workshops focus on developing specific skills in a guided setting.


Skill-Based Learning:

Workshops alternate between fundamental techniques (e.g., centring clay, coiling, glazing basics) and more advanced topics (e.g., altered forms, surface decoration).


Experienced Guidance:

Sessions are led by experienced student leaders or local artists, providing hands-on instruction and personalized feedback.


Creative Focus:

This is the perfect environment to try a new technique without the pressure of a full academic course.


Community Events


Price Range: Free to $15
Open to: Members and Non-Members
(Must Register to Attend)

When: Once A Month

These events are the heart of our community. They are designed to bring members together, foster friendships, and celebrate the season.


Social Making Sessions:

We host informal gatherings focused on communal creation, where members can work on projects while connecting with peers and sharing advice in a low-pressure environment, often accompanied by complimentary refreshments.


Club Community Builders

Provides opportunities for social, non-studio gatherings, including events like movie nights, themed holiday parties (Halloween, Christmas), or simple hangouts designed solely for bonding, decompression, and enjoying the club atmosphere.


Our Mission


Our mission is to foster a vibrant, low-stress community that breaks down the financial barriers to ceramic arts, offering all students the resources and instruction needed to transform clay into personal expression.
